# AI Pattern Rules

These rules exist because AI-generated text has recognizable patterns that spam filters detect and recipients instinctively distrust. Every variation must read like a real human typed it in 90 seconds without overthinking.

## Section 1 — Banned sentence openers

These words / phrases cannot start any variation:

- "Certainly" / "Absolutely" / "Of course"
- "Great question" / "That's a great point"
- "I'd be happy to" / "I'm glad you asked"
- "It's worth noting that"
- "It's important to note that"
- "Interestingly" / "Notably" / "Remarkably"
- "In today's [anything]" (e.g. "In today's market")
- "When it comes to"
- "In terms of"
- "As a matter of fact"
- "At the end of the day"
- "It goes without saying"

Start with the subject or the action. Get to the point.

## Section 2 — Banned filler phrases (replace with shorter form)

| Banned | Use instead |
|---|---|
| in order to | to |
| due to the fact that | because / since |
| for the purpose of | for / to |
| at this point in time | now |
| in the event that | if |
| in the near future | soon |
| with regard to / with respect to | about / on |
| a number of | some / several / actual number |
| the vast majority of | most |
| is able to / has the ability to | can |
| make a determination | decide |
| take into consideration | consider |
| on the basis of | based on |

## Section 3 — Banned transition stacking

Do not start sentences with:
- "Furthermore," / "Moreover," / "Additionally," / "Consequently,"
- "Nevertheless," / "Notwithstanding," / "In addition to this,"
- "On the other hand," / "Having said that," / "With that being said,"
- "That being said," / "Be that as it may,"

Use instead: "But", "So", "And", "Still", "Yet", "Plus" — or just start a new sentence with no transition at all.

## Section 4 — Banned summary / recap sentences

Do not use:
- "In summary," / "To summarize," / "All in all," / "In conclusion,"
- "Overall," / "The bottom line is"
- "To put it simply," / "Simply put," / "Long story short,"

Just end the email. The CTA is the ending. Do not recap.

## Section 5 — Banned enthusiasm words

Hard ban:

exciting, excited, thrilled, delighted, fantastic, wonderful, amazing, incredible, exceptional, outstanding, remarkable, game-changing, groundbreaking, revolutionary, leverage, unlock, empower, transform, elevate, optimize, robust, seamless, cutting-edge, holistic, synergy, ecosystem

## Section 6 — Banned empathy openers

- "I understand that..." / "I know how hard it can be to..."
- "I can imagine that..." / "I appreciate you taking the time..."
- "I hope this message finds you well" / "I hope you're doing well"
- "I trust you're having a great day"

Skip the pleasantry. First sentence = the reason you're emailing.

## Section 8 — Hedge words (avoid)

- "just" as a softener ("I just wanted to..." → "I wanted to...")
- "actually" / "basically" / "honestly" / "literally" (all filler)
- "perhaps" / "possibly" / "somewhat" / "relatively" / "arguably"
- "I think" / "I believe" / "it seems like" / "kind of" / "sort of"

Exception: "just" is fine when it means "only" ("It's just a 3-minute application").

## Section 9 — Parallel structure

Triple constructions are AI fingerprints. Vary the structure.

- Bad: "We help you save time, reduce costs, and grow revenue."
- Good: "We help you save time and cut costs."

## Section 10 — Sentence mechanics

- **Contractions mandatory** where natural. "I have" → "I've". "You will" → "You'll". "We would" → "we'd". "Do not" → "don't".
- **One idea per sentence.** Max 25 words. If a sentence exceeds 25 words, split it.
- **Front-load the important word.** Subject first, verb second, object third. Then context. (Amazon writing style.)
  - Bad: "Based on what I found in SBA records, it appears you paid down your loan."
  - Good: "You paid down your loan, I found this in SBA records."
- **Vary sentence length.** Mix at least one short sentence (2-5 words) per email when natural. Short sentences work as reactions: "Good sign." / "Makes sense." / "That stood out."
- **No mirrored sentence starts.** Never start 2+ sentences in a row with the same word (especially "I", "We", "This", "That").

## Section 11 — CTA rules

- **CTA must be a question in all 5 variations.** The last line before the signature is a question, not a statement.
  - Bad: "Let me know your thoughts." / "I look forward to hearing from you."
  - Good: "Want me to send the 3-minute application?" / "Does this sound like a fit?"

## Section 12 — Sign-off

Banned sign-off clichés:
- "Looking forward to connecting with you"
- "I appreciate your time and consideration"
- "Please don't hesitate to reach out"
- "I look forward to hearing from you"

Acceptable: "Talk soon," / "Best," / "Thanks," / just the name.

## Section 13 — Protected elements (never swap)

These must stay verbatim across all 5 variations:

- **Variables** — `{{firstName}}`, `{COMPANY}`, etc. Keep exact bracket style and casing.
- **The word "help"** — never change to assist / aid / support.
- **Financial terms** — "paid down", "revenue", "profit", "equity".
- **Medical terms** — "chronic", "acute", "symptom", "diagnosed".
- **Legal terms** — "shall", "may", "liable", "contract".
- **When in doubt, keep the original word.**

## Section 14 — Good vs bad phrases

Good (use these):
- "Let me know" (not "Hit me up")
- "I can send" (not "I can shoot over")
- "Want to chat?" (not "Wanna talk?")
- "Happy to" (not "Down to")
- Observation verbs: saw, spotted, found, noticed, checked, came across
- Delivery verbs: send, share, give, show

Bad (never use):
- shoot over, fire over, hit me up, ping me
- wanna, gonna, got a sec, sound good, down to
- pumped, stoked, sweet, no worries, circle back
- assist, utilize, approximately, demonstrate, facilitate
- subsequently, nevertheless, consequently, furthermore
